### Course Description

This course introduces Compilers and Interpreters: what they are, and how they work.  We cover both theory and practice for: Lexing, Parsing, ASTs, Interpretation, Codegen and Optimization.  The class project builds a Compiler for a subset of the C programming language, generating ARM-32 machine code.

### Grading

Homework: 30%, Projects: 40%, Midterm: 15%, Final: 15%

A scale of 90s (3.5‐4.0), 80s (2.5‐3.4), 70s (1.5‐2.4), 60s (0.5‐1.4) is a rough guide.

### Textbook

The lectures will cover all of the material needed to understand the course.  However, students may find it helpful to refer to a text book.  There are dozens of good books available on compilers.  For example:

[Engineering a Compiler](https://smile.amazon.com/Engineering-Compiler-Keith-Cooper/dp/012088478X/ref=sr_1_1?keywords=cooper+and+torczon+compiler&qid=1571776415&sr=8-1), by Cooper & Torczon, Second Edition, 2011.  This is solid and understandable, with lots of practical advice.  It covers much more than needed for CSS-448.
