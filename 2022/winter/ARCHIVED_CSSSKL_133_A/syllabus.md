## Requirements

**Lab Requirements:   **Labs will be assigned and turned in using **Canvas.**  

**Lab Grading:**  The lab is**** graded on both**engagement and effort** , not on the correctness of the output. Each lab is worth four (4) points where points are achieved as follows:

  
_**Pre-Lab Activity for the coming week:**_  
1 pt for the **Pre-lab activity** is completed and turned in prior to the deadline  
  
 _**Lab Activity for the current week:**_  


3pts - **All parts** attempted and **acceptable efforts**  
2pts \- **Not all sections** of the lab were attempted but the **acceptable effort** was made   
1pts - **Missing majority of the lab** , or no efforts shown   
0pts - absent work, no effort made (e.g. just starter code files), Academic Dishonesty  
  


**Lab due date:  **The lab report will be due the Wednesday following the lab it was assigned at 11:59 pm.  
**Pre-Lab due date:** The pre-lab activity will be due Thursday at 11:59 pm just prior to the next lab.  
  


**Lab 1 Exception:** There is no Pre-Lab for lab 1, so the Lab Activity will count for all 4 points.

_**Resubmission Policy:**_

After receiving the grades for the labs, students will be provided an extended deadline for resubmission if they are not satisfied with their scores. The resubmission deadline will be the same date as the deadline for your next lab i.e. the subsequent Wednesday. If you did not submit the assignment on time, you do not qualify for resubmission and will receive no credit on the assignment, so please be sure to always turn in something by the first due date. The assignment will close after the resubmission date has passed.

_**Late work Policy:**_

Late lab submissions will not be accepted, except in the case of one of the following reasons:

  1. Illness
  2. Bereavement
  3. Government Obligations
  4. University Business
  5. Widespread Technical Problems



**No other late work will be accepted for any reason. Please send me an email if you need to turn in an assignment late for one of the listed reasons.** Because of the resubmission policy, even incomplete work may be regraded for full credit. Therefore, it is in the student's best interest to **submit the work they have on the due date.**

## Course Policies

### Student Conduct, Plagiarism and Cheating

It is your responsibility to know and uphold the _Student Conduct Code for the University of Washington_ , available at [_http://www.uwb.edu/students/policies/_](http://www.uwb.edu/students/policies/) 

To simplify the code in the extreme, don't cheat. If you feel the need to violate your (or another student's) ethics in order to keep up with the class, please just talk to me and your 142 professors. In the long run, relying upon shortcuts like plagiarism will have extremely dangerous consequences in the world outside of school; you have a responsibility to yourself, your future clients, and those around you to establish sound ethical behavior during your time in school (and sometimes that includes having the maturity to engage with sub-optimal results). You will receive a zero and/or be referred to the University for any coursework for which you are discovered cheating or plagiarizing. If you are caught cheating for the initial submission, you may resubmit per the resubmission policy, but the maximum grade you can achieve is a 2 out of 3 for a full lab assignment.

### Engagement and Attendance

Attendance is encouraged but not mandatory. Efforts will be made to ensure that if for whatever reason, you are unable to attend lab, the relevant course content will be made otherwise available.

### Inclement Weather Policy

If there is a weather event severe enough to make the transit to campus dangerous (extreme storms, flooding, etc.) the class will be canceled or moved to remote learning. If the class is not canceled but you personally will be unable to attend due to inclement weather, please inform me.
