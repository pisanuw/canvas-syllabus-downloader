Course Description

Games are simulations. Games are narratives. Games are puzzles. Games are advertisements. Games are commentary on social issues. Games are all those and more. Games are a form of rhetoric, a chance for the game designer to present to the player their world view.  We will explore how games tackle social issues, everything from climate change to politics to education to cancer. You will play games, dissect their meaning and build your own games. Together, we will explore computational thinking and storytelling through the lens of game design.



Course Learning Objectives

At the end of this course, students will be able to: 

  1. Identify elements of games and analyze dynamic game systems
  2. Understand the hidden messages in games and how games shape players
  3. Create an original game and test it

Late Work Policy

Other than the final group project, you can re-submit your reflection papers to be regraded within two weeks of the original deadline. If you do submit for regrading, the maximum additional points you can get are 50% of the points that you initially lost. For example, if you initially got 6/10 and you resubmit, your maximum possible grade is 8/10.

 

If you do not submit your initial work on-time, your initial grade will be 0/10, so even with resubmission, you can only get to 5/10. Make sure you submit your assignments on time.

 

There is no resubmission of the final group project.

 

Participation Policy

Since positive participation is vital for a successful experience, please arrive on time for each class. Late arrivals interrupt our in-progress activities and discussions. If you must miss a class session, let me know as soon as possible. “Positive participation” is evaluated through a combination of your contributions, attitude, preparedness, focus, behavior, and effort. Content Warnings Policy

Some of the material that we’ll be covering may be triggering to folks who have experienced trauma (arising from violent or sexual assault, domestic abuse, racism, suicide, military combat, and more). In an effort to create an accessible learning space, I will do my best to tell you all in advance on the course schedule and prior to class sessions when a reading, film, lecture discusses potentially distressing topics. I also encourage you to practice self-care before, during and after these materials have been presented. If you find that you are struggling to process the material we cover, please let me know as soon as you feel able and consider reaching out to our Counseling Center or Victim Advocate for additional support.
