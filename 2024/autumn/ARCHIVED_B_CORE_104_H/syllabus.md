## **Course Description**

_Games are simulations. Games are narratives. Games are puzzles. Games are advertisements. Games are commentary on social issues. Games are all those and more. Games are a form of rhetoric, a chance for the game designer to present to the player their world view.   We will explore how games tackle social issues, everything from climate change to politics to education to cancer. You will play games, dissect their meaning and build your own games. Together, we will explore computational thinking and storytelling through the lens of game design._

  
  


## **Course Learning Objectives******

At the end of this course, students will be able to: 

  1. Identify elements of games and analyze dynamic game systems
  2. Understand the hidden messages in games and how games shape players
  3. Create an original game and test it

## **Late Work Policy**

Other than the final group project, you can re-submit your reflection papers to be regraded within two weeks of the original deadline. If you do submit for regrading, the maximum additional points you can get are 50% of the points that you initially lost. For example, if you initially got 6/10 and you resubmit, your maximum possible grade is 8/10.

 

If you do not submit your initial work on-time, your initial grade will be 0/10, so even with resubmission, you can only get to 5/10. Make sure you submit your assignments on time.

 

There is no resubmission of the final group project.
